Consent To Participate

1. Purpose of the Study

This research is being conducted by Dr. Naeemul Hassan, Dr. Jordan Boyd-Graber, and Yoo Yeon Sung at the University of Maryland, College Park. This survey is made to construct a data set for academic research to prevent the use of deceptive techniques in headlines. These techniques often trick the readers into clicking on videos or links that then direct users to misleading and disappointing contents. Your survey answers will be used effectively in our research to help the cyber-community users to avoid misleading headlines.

2. Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to do the following:

  1. Click “save and next” button if you have approximately 30 minutes to complete the survey.
  2. Follow the instructions and answer the questions as you watch the given video. The content of videos include categories of war, government, politics, education, health, environment, economy, business, fashion, and entertainment.
  3. A sample question you will be asked is “Assuming that the information provided by the video is correct, how would you rate the headline?”
  4. Complete the survey.

3. Potential Risks and Discomforts

There are no identified risks for participating in the study. If the video is considered sensitive or cause any discomfort, you can refuse to answer any question in the survey and quit at any time. However, if you quit before completing the survey, you will not be compensated. You may also report your discomfort to one of the investigators via email: cjlabumd482@gmail.com.

4. Potential Benefits

You will be provided with new knowledge of how clickbaits are prevalent in social media community and how it may harm those who are not aware of its danger in terms of security and information distribution. Potential benefits include helping social media scholars, journalists, industry professionals, and the general public to understand which news headlines are clickbaits and avoid them. We hope that, in the future, media researchers and journalists might benefit from this study through an improved understanding of clickbaity headlines and developing an intensified censoring system to enhance cybersecurity.

5. Confidentiality

All responses to this survey are anonymous. Any potential loss of confidentiality will be minimized by storing your information on a password protected computer. Survey responses will be saved in UMD Box with a password protection with access only by the study researchers. Any anonymous demographic data such as age and gender will remain confidential and will be kept safeguarded only by the study researchers on UMD Box with a password protection for a period not to exceed one year at which time the dataset will be deleted from UMD Box.

6. Compensation

You will not be compensated for the qualification test questions that you have completed beforehand, whether you have answered it correctly or incorrectly. However, you are qualified to participate in the actual survey because you have answered them correctly. You will be paid for completing the survey through an online crowd sourcing platform. You will be paid $1 for completing the survey, assuming you have spent 10 minutes. You will be responsible for any taxes assessed on the compensation. There will be NO attention check question in the survey and you will be compensated for your work.

7. Right to Withdraw and Questions

Your participation in this research is completely voluntary. You may choose not to take part at all. If you decide to participate in this research, you may stop participating at any time. If you decide not to participate in this study or if you stop participating at any time, you will not be penalized or lose any benefits to which you otherwise qualify.

Statement Headlines

Please choose Factual if the statement is making an assertion and could be verified whether it is true or false. Additionally, you should assess the statement to be factual according to the overall message, not the phrase in the quotation mark only.

ex) Biden was not elected in 2020

ex) She called me a “pretty boy”

ex) Trump has 10 children

ex) Trump to Biden: 'You're the Puppet'

ex) She provided tips for making oatmeal


Please choose Opinionated if the statement is giving an opinion or judgement. If there is any component in the statement that could be understood differently depending on the person’s experience, it should be categorized as opinionated.

ex) Best ways to make oatmeal (The word ‘best’ is open to interpretation)

ex) The power of healthy food (The word ‘healthy’ is open to interpretation)

ex) Vulgar language from Trump (The word ‘vulgar’ is open to interpretation)

ex) Why you must vote (The sentence is specifically asking for person’s opinion)

ex) 5 minutes of truth (The word ‘truth’ may imply different things depending on your experience)


Please choose Neither if the statement is not making an assertion or the statement is not factual or opinionated. For example, “Teacher’s advice” should be categorized as Neither, while “She was advised by a teacher” should be categorized as Factual. Here are some examples when you should select Neither.

1. The statement is an objective phrase

ex) Great Depression

ex) Google’s new phone

ex) Trump’s wife

ex) The power of food

ex) George Bush on election 2016

2. The statement seems to be opinionated, but you cannot figure out what the opinion is.

ex) Tips for making oatmeal

ex) Teacher’s advice

ex) 5 sentences from your teacher

3. The statement is an instruction sentence that is objective.

ex) Ask questions before you depart

ex) Request for approval, do not demand for it

ex) Make your own coconut milk
